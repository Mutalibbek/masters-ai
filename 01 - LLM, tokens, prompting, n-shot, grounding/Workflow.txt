Artificial Intelligence (AI) has moved beyond a futuristic buzzword and into practical applications that impact our daily lives. Understanding AI and Machine Learning
A significant focus of the lecture was differentiating between Artificial Intelligence (AI), Machine Learning (ML), and related technologies. Mr. Shulha explained that AI aims to mimic human behavior, while machine learning allows systems to learn from data and improve performance over time. He introduced concepts like deep learning, natural language processing (NLP), and large language models (LLMs), such as GPT.

He shared an analogy: “Think of AI as anything that seeks to replicate human intelligence, and machine learning as the engine that drives this intelligence, learning from patterns in data.”

One of the most compelling parts of the lecture was the focus on generative AI. Mr. Shulha explained that tools like GPT, Stable Diffusion, and OpenAI’s ecosystem are revolutionizing how software and education operate. These tools allow engineers to automate tasks, generate content, and optimize processes.

He discussed the challenges of data privacy, model training, and biases, citing real-world scenarios where AI outputs were misused.

He introduced the course structure, highlighting that students will engage in practical tasks like creating AI prototypes, deploying APIs, and working with tools like PyTorch for machine learning experiments. The lecture was a clear reminder of AI’s immense potential in reshaping industries and lives.
